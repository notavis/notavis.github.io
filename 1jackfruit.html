The
Jackfruit

Jackfruit is a tropical fruit that is native to Southeast Asia, but is now cultivated
in many other regions around the world. It is the largest tree-borne fruit in the
world and can weigh up to 80 pounds. Jackfruit has a spiky, greenish-yellow
exterior and a creamy yellow flesh inside that is divided into segments. It has
a sweet and fruity flavor and can be eaten ripe or unripe. When unripe, jackfruit
has a meaty texture and is often used as a vegetarian or vegan meat substitute.
When ripe, jackfruit is juicy and has a flavor similar to a mix of pineapple and
mango. Jackfruit is high in fiber, vitamin C, and other essential nutrients. It is
also a good source of antioxidants and phytonutrients. Some popular uses of
jackfruit include curries, stews, and tacos.
