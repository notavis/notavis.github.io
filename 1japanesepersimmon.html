The
Persimmon

Persimmon is a fruit that is native to China and Japan, but is now cultivated
in many other regions around the world. It has a round or oblong shape and
can range in colour from yellow-orange to deep red. Persimmons have a
sweet and juicy flesh that can be eaten fresh or used in a variety of dishes,
such as jams, salads, and desserts. There are two main types of persimmons:
astringent and non-astringent. Astringent persimmons are high in tannins,
which can make them taste bitter and astringent until they are fully ripe.
Non-astringent persimmons are sweeter and less astringent, and can be eaten
when still firm. Persimmons are high in fiber, vitamin A, and vitamin C, and are
also a good source of other essential nutrients. Some popular types of
persimmons include Fuyu, Hachiya, and Sharon fruit.
